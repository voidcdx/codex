Warframe Damage Calculation Wiki — Quantization Scale Analysis
=============================================================

QUESTION
--------
Does the wiki define "base damage" (for the quantization scale) as the weapon's
total base damage across all pellets, or the per-pellet amount? Is there any
worked example involving a shotgun, multishot weapon, or pellet count?


SCALE FORMULA (wiki line 172)
------------------------------
    Scale = Modded Base Damage / 32


QUANTIZATION FORMULA (wiki line 174)
--------------------------------------
    Quantized Damage Type Value = Round(Total Damage Type Value / Scale) × Scale


WORKED EXAMPLE (wiki lines 176-178)
-------------------------------------
Setup:
  "if you have a weapon with a listed damage distribution of
   30 Impact, 30 Puncture, and 40 Slash, the total damage is 100."

Scale:
  "The value of a scale will then be 100 ÷ 32 = 3.125."

Per-type quantization:
  Impact:   30 ÷ 3.125 = 9.6  → rounds to 10  → 10 × 3.125 = 31.25
  Puncture: 30 ÷ 3.125 = 9.6  → rounds to 10  → 10 × 3.125 = 31.25
  Slash:    40 ÷ 3.125 = 12.8 → rounds to 13  → 13 × 3.125 = 40.625

Against a Charger (+50% Slash, Infested health):
  31.25 + 31.25 + 40.625 × 1.5 = 123.4375  (displayed as 123)


SHOTGUN / PELLET COUNT / MULTISHOT — SEARCH RESULT
----------------------------------------------------
No matches found for: pellet, per-pellet, shotgun, Sobek, Tigris,
Arca Plasmor, or any formula dividing by pellet count.

The only multishot reference in the file is in the Arsenal display formula:
  Arsenal Total Damage = Base Damage × [...] × [Base weapon multishot × (1 + Multishot Bonuses)]
This is explicitly for the Arsenal stat display, NOT for quantization.


CONCLUSION
----------
The wiki defines "base damage" as the weapon's TOTAL listed damage (the sum
shown in the Arsenal, e.g. 100 in the example). There is no per-pellet
distinction anywhere in the quantization section.

The scale is always:
    Scale = total modded base damage / 32

No division by pellet count. No shotgun example. The current calculator
implementation (using the weapon's total base damage as the quantization
denominator) is consistent with the wiki documentation.
